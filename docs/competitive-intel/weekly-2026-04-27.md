## 1. arXiv / Academic

### No new papers in the last 7 days

No preprints matching "3D print orientation optimization," "build direction optimization," "support structure minimization," "Gauss map + additive manufacturing," or "Hopf fibration + 3D printing" appeared on arXiv in the April 20–27 window.

**Background item to monitor (not new this week):**

- **Shin et al., arXiv:2502.20343 (Feb 2025)** — "Topology Optimization for Multi-Axis Additive Manufacturing Considering Overhang and Anisotropy"  
  *What it is:* Space-time topology optimization that jointly optimizes density distribution and build orientation for multi-axis printers, with closed-form overhang constraints and material anisotropy modeling.  
  *Relevance:* Attacks a harder problem (multi-axis + topology co-design) than orientIQ's scope (fixed topology, single-axis FDM/SLA), so it is not a direct overlap. No threat to orientIQ's positioning.  
  Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.20343

- **Springer 2025: "Comprehensive additive manufacturing cost reduction through simultaneous topology and build orientation optimization"**  
  *What it is:* Uses four cost proxies — build height, surface area, overhang area, support volume — in a multi-objective formulation; reports 11–16% material reduction and 12–54% print-time reduction vs. sequential optimization.  
  *Relevance:* The objective set is nearly identical to orientIQ's (overhang, build height, surface quality, support volume). This is a research proof-of-concept, not a shipped tool, and it couples topology optimization — meaning it requires design-stage integration. orientIQ targets existing/fixed meshes at slicer time, a different deployment context. Worth tracking if the authors release code or a tool.  
  Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8
