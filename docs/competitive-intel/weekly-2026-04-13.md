## 1. Academic Papers

### Confirmed recent (2025–2026, not necessarily last 7 days)

**Crispo & Kim — "Combined topology and build orientation optimization for support structure minimization in additive manufacturing"**  
*Structural and Multidisciplinary Optimization, Vol. 68, Art. 216 (2025)*  
- **What it is:** Gradient-based joint optimization of part topology and build direction, targeting support structure *volume* (not just overhang area). Uses differentiable mapping of element densities from a rotated FEM mesh onto a structured print-direction-aligned mesh. Validated on an aircraft bearing bracket; showed 5–54% support material reduction vs. overhang-area-only minimization.  
- **Competitive relevance:** Low near-term threat. This is design-time topology optimization — it modifies the part geometry itself. orientIQ works post-design on a fixed mesh. Methodologically they share multi-objective framing and overhang sensitivity, but they solve different problems for different users. Watch if Autodesk or Siemens productizes this kind of combined TO+orientation into slicer workflows.  
- **Source:** [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00158-025-04124-6)

**Anonymous — "Comprehensive additive manufacturing cost reduction through simultaneous topology and build orientation optimization"**  
*Progress in Additive Manufacturing (2026)*  
- **What it is:** Four-objective cost model (build height, surface area, overhang area, support volume) used simultaneously in topology+orientation optimization. Claims 11–16% material reduction and 12–54% print-time reduction vs. sequential/single-objective baselines. Verified on an aircraft seat leg.  
- **Competitive relevance:** Same design-time vs. mesh-prep distinction as above. The four-objective framing parallels orientIQ's multi-objective Pareto work. No algorithmic overlap with S² search or Hopf fibration.  
- **Source:** [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8)

**No results found this week for:** "Hopf fibration" + 3D printing, "Gauss map" + additive manufacturing, or any arXiv preprint using S²-search for orientation. No new direct algorithmic competition on orientIQ's specific mathematical approach.

## 2. Slicer Releases

### Autodesk Netfabb 2026
- **What it is:** Annual release. Redesigned UI with easier access to support generation and orientation optimization tools. Improved nesting/packing for powder bed fusion. Deeper 3MF format support. Updated automation scripts for build preparation.  
- **Competitive relevance:** Moderate. Netfabb is the incumbent enterprise tool; this release tightens integration with Fusion and positions Autodesk as a DfAM-to-production pipeline. The orientation improvements appear to be UI/workflow refinements rather than algorithmic advances. No indication they are using Riemannian gradient descent, S² search, or multi-objective Pareto analysis. orientIQ's mathematical rigor and Python API remain differentiated.  
- **Source:** [3D Printing Industry](https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/autodesk-updates-netfabb-with-enhanced-workflow-tools-and-new-3mf-support-in-2026-release-238996/)

## 3. Startup / Company News

### Castor — CLOSED (October 2025)
- **What it is:** Israeli AM decision-support software startup Castor (8 years old, ~$5.9M raised) filed for court-ordered liquidation in July 2025 and confirmed full closure in October 2025. CEO Omer Blaier cited mounting debt (~$2.3M USD), a U.S. manufacturing R&D slowdown starting 2023, and reduced AM investment budgets.  
- **Competitive relevance:** High opportunity. Castor was the most direct analogue to orientIQ — automated geometry-based orientation analysis for industrial AM. Their closure leaves a gap in the market, particularly among mid-market industrial users who had trialed or budgeted for this category of software. Their customers are now without a tool.  
- **Sources:** [3DPrint.com](https://3dprint.com/319042/israeli-3d-printing-software-startup-castor-files-for-liquidation/) · [3D Printing Industry](https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/castor-closes-after-eight-years-as-founder-confirms-companys-end-246199/)

## Analyst Notes

- **Castor's exit is the most actionable signal.** Their former prospects — industrial manufacturers evaluating automated orientation tools — are a natural pipeline for orientIQ. Worth considering outreach or positioning content targeting this audience.
- **Academic work on combined topology+orientation** is converging on multi-objective formulations similar to orientIQ's Pareto front, but all published work requires modifying the geometry (topology optimization). orientIQ's value proposition on fixed meshes remains uncontested in the academic literature.
- **Slicer vendors** (Bambu, Prusa) continue shipping heuristic auto-orient with no mathematical rigor. The gap between their approach and orientIQ's S²+Riemannian gradient descent is widening in orientIQ's favor as slicers prioritize hardware/material features.
- **Netfabb 2026** is the only enterprise incumbent making orientation workflow improvements. Monitor their Spring 2026 feature set; if they announce gradient-based or Pareto-front orientation features, escalate to a deeper review.
