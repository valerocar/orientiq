## 1. arXiv / Research

**Net finding: Nothing published this specific week in orientIQ's core areas.**

The most recent relevant paper found is ~3 weeks old:

- **[Multi-Axis Additive Manufacturing for Customized Automotive Components](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12236)** (arXiv 2604.12236, April 14 2026)
  - Summary: Uses multi-axis DLP to orient a robotic arm so resin layers cure at varying angles, eliminating supports on automotive organic geometries.
  - Competitive relevance: Low. Addresses 5-axis robotic printing, a different regime than FDM/SLA part-on-plate orientation. The core problem (support minimization) overlaps but the solution space (continuous tool-path reorientation vs. fixed build direction) is orthogonal to orientIQ's S²-reduction approach.
  - Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12236

Earlier 2026 papers (outside this week's window, for awareness):

- **[Comprehensive AM Cost Reduction via Simultaneous Topology + Build Orientation Optimization](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8)** (Springer Progress in AM, Feb 2026)
  - Summary: Joint topology/orientation optimizer using four cost proxies (build height, surface area, overhang area, support volume); reports 11–16% material reduction and 12–54% print-time reduction vs. sequential approaches.
  - Competitive relevance: Medium. This is the closest academic work to orientIQ's objective function. It uses the same four proxies but frames them as a topology problem (changing geometry), not a fixed-geometry orientation problem. No Hopf reduction — pure gradient-free heuristics. Worth tracking to see if a fast fixed-geometry sub-routine is published.
  - Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8

No papers mentioning "Hopf fibration" + 3D printing or "Gauss map" + additive manufacturing were found anywhere in 2026. orientIQ's mathematical framing remains differentiating.
