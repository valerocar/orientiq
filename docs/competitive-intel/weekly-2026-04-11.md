## 1. Academic Papers

**No new arXiv papers this week** on "3D print orientation optimization",
"build direction optimization", "support structure minimization", "Gauss map +
additive manufacturing", or "Hopf fibration + 3D printing" were returned for
April 4–11, 2026. Searches surfaced only earlier work.

**Background papers worth tracking (published 2025, not this week):**

- **"Combined topology and build orientation optimization for support structure
  minimization"** (Structural and Multidisciplinary Optimization, Oct 2025)
  — Simultaneous topology + orientation co-optimization; reports 5–54%
  support reduction. Competitive because it frames multi-objective orientation
  as a topology problem rather than a standalone search, which is a different
  angle than orientIQ's mesh-agnostic approach. Not a direct threat to the
  library but relevant to how academic citations will frame the space.
  [Link](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00158-025-04124-6)

- **"Comprehensive additive manufacturing cost reduction through simultaneous
  topology and build orientation optimization"** (Progress in Additive
  Manufacturing, 2025) — Four-objective cost model (build height, surface
  area, overhang area, support volume); 11–16% material reduction, 12–54%
  print-time reduction vs. sequential methods. Uses genetic algorithms, not
  Riemannian descent — slower but handles coupled topology/orientation.
  [Link](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8)

- **arXiv:2502.20343 — "Topology Optimization for Multi-Axis Additive
  Manufacturing Considering Overhang and Anisotropy"** (Feb 2025)
  — Extends orientation work to multi-axis (5-axis) AM; not directly
  competing with FDM/SLA single-axis orientation but worth watching if
  orientIQ expands scope.
  [Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.20343)

**Assessment:** No Hopf fibration or S² geometry work appeared in AM context
this week. orientIQ's mathematical framing remains academically unduplicated.

  with orientation changes was indexed.
- A prior fix addressed auto-orientation GUI/CLI inconsistency (undated).

  [Releases](https://github.com/bambulab/BambuStudio/releases)

### Netfabb 2026 (released April 22, 2025 — background context)
- Redesigned UI simplifies access to orientation and support generation tools;
  new support-angle controls prevent deformation. "True Shape" 2D/3D packing
  command added.
- **Netfabb Ultimate retired** along with Selective Space Structures,
  Optimization Utility, and Lattice Topology tools — Autodesk is pruning
  advanced optimization features, not expanding them.
- Competitive read: Autodesk is moving away from deep orientation math, which
  is a mild tailwind for specialist tools like orientIQ.
  [Coverage](https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/autodesk-updates-netfabb-with-enhanced-workflow-tools-and-new-3mf-support-in-2026-release-238996/)

### Materialise Magics 30
- Scheduled Spring 2026 (alpha available, GA not yet shipped). Features
  confirmed so far: new Lattice Module, enhanced Nester (build-plate packing),
  nTop implicit-geometry import, dark-mode UI. **No orientation-algorithm
  changes mentioned.**
- Competitive read: Magics remains the industrial incumbent for build
  preparation. The Nester improvements (automatic build-plate packing) are
  adjacent to orientation but not the same problem. Watch the GA release for
  any auto-orient announcements.
  [What's New](https://www.materialise.com/en/industrial/software/magics-data-build-preparation/whats-new)
  [Alpha notes](https://help.materialise.com/magics-30-alpha)

### Chitubox
- Added dental automation (dental orientation + dental model hollowing) and
  .glb/.gltf import. Domain-specific (dental resin), not a threat to general
  orientation optimization.
  [Changelog](https://www.chitubox.com/en/index/changelog)

## 3. Startups & Commercial Tools

  **July 2025**, citing mounting debt and reduced industry momentum.
