### No new arXiv hits this week

### Crispo & Kim — combined topology + orientation co-optimization (2025/2026)
Two papers from the same authors, both in Springer journals:

- **"Combined topology and build orientation optimization for support structure minimization in additive manufacturing"** — *Structural and Multidisciplinary Optimization*, 2025. Jointly optimizes part topology and build direction to eliminate overhangs without explicit support structures.
  [https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00158-025-04124-6](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00158-025-04124-6)

- **"Comprehensive additive manufacturing cost reduction through simultaneous topology and build orientation optimization"** — *Progress in Additive Manufacturing*, 2026. Uses build height + surface area + overhang area + support volume as a combined cost model; reports 11–16% material reduction and 12–54% print-time reduction vs. sequential optimization.
  [https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40964-025-01520-8)

**Competitive relevance — low direct threat, useful signal.** These papers solve a different (harder) problem: they redesign the part geometry while simultaneously choosing build direction. orientIQ operates on a fixed mesh. However, the objective decomposition they use (build height + overhang area + support volume) closely mirrors orientIQ's objective set, and the 12–54% print-time improvement claim is the kind of number that will appear in competitor marketing. Worth understanding the method in case a future orientIQ feature targets topology-aware pre-processing.

## 2. Slicer Releases

### Netfabb 2026.0 — released April 22, 2026
Major annual release. Orientation-adjacent changes: improved packing via a new **True Shape** algorithm designed for serial production (high-throughput, multi-platform distribution). Support generation additions are process-specific: new HP Metal Jet S100 shadow-support and setter scripts. No changes to the core orientation optimization algorithm were announced.
[https://forums.autodesk.com/t5/netfabb-forum/autodesk-netfabb-2026-is-now-available/td-p/13437539](https://forums.autodesk.com/t5/netfabb-forum/autodesk-netfabb-2026-is-now-available/td-p/13437539)
[https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/autodesk-updates-netfabb-with-enhanced-workflow-tools-and-new-3mf-support-in-2026-release-238996/](https://3dprintingindustry.com/news/autodesk-updates-netfabb-with-enhanced-workflow-tools-and-new-3mf-support-in-2026-release-238996/)

**Competitive relevance — neutral.** The True Shape packing improvement is a build-plate packing feature (different problem from single-part orientation). No evidence Autodesk is advancing the orientation algorithm itself.

### Materialise Magics 30 — upcoming Spring 2026, not yet released
Forthcoming release adds ID-based licensing. The orientation story is Ansys Simulation integration: simulation-in-the-loop to pick orientations that minimize warping and residual stress for metal AM.
[https://www.materialise.com/en/industrial/software/magics-data-build-preparation/whats-new](https://www.materialise.com/en/industrial/software/magics-data-build-preparation/whats-new)

**Competitive relevance — monitor.** Materialise has always had orientation tools; simulation-based orientation selection (driven by FEA stress/distortion) is a different signal than geometric minimization. It targets metal AM process engineers rather than the general FDM/SLA user. Not a direct threat to orientIQ's geometric approach today, but simulation-guided orientation is a direction worth tracking.

### PrusaSlicer 3.0 — pre-release, Spring 2026 window
Still not out. Auto-orient for FFF printing remains an open GitHub feature request (#13091). SLA has three orientation styles since v2.4; FFF does not.
[https://github.com/prusa3d/PrusaSlicer/issues/13091](https://github.com/prusa3d/PrusaSlicer/issues/13091)

**Competitive relevance — opportunity.** PrusaSlicer's continued absence of FFF auto-orient is a gap orientIQ can reference as a positioning point.

### Bambu Studio V2.6.0 / V2.6.1 Beta
Most recent release centers on a new Filament Manager. Auto Orient exists in the product but no changes to the orientation algorithm were announced in the V2.6 cycle.
[https://github.com/bambulab/BambuStudio/releases](https://github.com/bambulab/BambuStudio/releases)
